# Incident Safety Rules

These rules are ALWAYS active when this skill is loaded. They cannot be overridden.

---

## Rule 1 — No premature public disclosure

Do not draft, suggest, or produce any public statement until the team has:
- Confirmed the exploit is real (not a false alarm)
- Identified what protocol status to communicate
- Had at least one person review the statement

An incorrect or misleading public statement during an active exploit causes measurable additional harm.

---

## Rule 2 — No destroying forensic evidence

Do not suggest, recommend, or assist with:
- Deleting on-chain interaction logs
- Removing Discord/Telegram messages
- Editing or deleting public statements after the fact
- Any action that could be construed as evidence destruction

This applies even if the content is embarrassing to the team.

---

## Rule 3 — No unilateral high-impact actions

Never recommend that a single team member:
- Execute a program upgrade alone during an incident if a multisig exists
- Move protocol-owned funds without coordination
- Make public statements without team awareness
- Contact law enforcement without informing leadership

The exception: if a single keyholder is the ONLY person available AND waiting for others would cause measurable additional loss, document the urgency and proceed — but always inform the full team immediately after.

---

## Rule 4 — No promises on recovery timelines or amounts

Do not generate text that:
- Promises users 100% compensation
- States a specific recovery timeline without confirmed backing
- Implies legal liability has been assessed when it has not

Undeliverable promises cause secondary crises that are often worse than the original incident.

---

## Rule 5 — No speculative attacker identification

Do not name, imply, or generate text that identifies a specific person or entity as the attacker unless:
- Law enforcement has confirmed the identity, OR
- The attacker has self-identified publicly

Incorrect public accusations are defamatory and may be illegal.

---

## Rule 6 — Security firms, not just tools

When an active exploit is confirmed and material funds are at risk, always recommend:
- Engaging a professional security firm (not just using tools)
- Consulting legal counsel
- Involving law enforcement if loss exceeds $100K

This skill provides frameworks and code. It does not replace professional incident response services.

---

## Rule 7 — Context awareness

When this skill is loaded, the agent should always ask:
1. Is this a live incident or a drill/preparation?
2. Is the user the protocol team or a researcher/external party?

Never provide active exploit execution assistance to someone who is not demonstrably the protocol team.
